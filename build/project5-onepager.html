Headline finding
EV/Sales tracks Damodaran's R&D/lease-adjusted operating margin better than the reported margin (R² 0.425 vs 0.406) across 84 non-financial industries — the market looks partly through R&D expensing. The adjustment reranks only 1/84 by ≥5 places (ρ = 0.998): the distortion is concentrated in the high-R&D tail.

The thesis (one claim)

Reported margins misprice the R&D-heavy tail in cross-industry screening — and the equity market already prices the adjusted figure.

So what: don't screen software / semis / pharma on reported operating margin or P/E. Non-circular by design: R&D-adjusted margins are Damodaran's own construction, so we never regress them on R&D intensity — the empirical test points at a market outcome (EV/Sales) instead. A lint blocks the circular pairing.

Engineering signals

  • Join on a surrogate id, never the raw string — catches Damodaran's real Heathcare typo; fails loudly on any unresolved name.
  • Vintage dimension — single snapshot → panel by design; ASC 842 / TCJA §174 break flags.
  • 15 tests + firm-count anchor (5,994) + anti-circularity lint, all green.
  • Right-sized — 8 facts, not 30; proportionality over gold-plating.

What it can & cannot show

  • Value-weighted industry aggregates (n≤94) — the "industry portfolio", not a typical firm.
  • Association, not causation — ecological fallacy, single snapshot.
  • Capitalized R&D is Damodaran's estimate, not GAAP.
  • US-only; Total Market rows stripped before every statistic.
